What is Fixed Interest Rate?

A fixed interest rate remains constant throughout the loan tenure. Your EMI amount does not change even if RBI repo rates increase or decrease.

Advantages of Fixed Rate

  • EMI remains stable
  • Better financial planning
  • No sudden EMI shock

Disadvantages

  • Usually higher than floating rate
  • Does not benefit when interest rates fall

What is Floating Interest Rate?

A floating interest rate changes based on RBI repo rate or market conditions. Your EMI or tenure may increase or decrease over time.

Advantages of Floating Rate

  • Usually lower starting rate
  • Benefits when interest rates fall
  • No major foreclosure charges (for home loans)

Disadvantages

  • EMI may increase unexpectedly
  • Loan tenure can extend

When Should You Choose Fixed Interest Rate?

  • If interest rates are expected to rise
  • If you prefer predictable EMIs
  • If you have tight monthly budget

When Should You Choose Floating Interest Rate?

  • If interest rates are high currently
  • If you expect rates to fall
  • If loan tenure is long (15â€“30 years)

Impact on EMI

With floating rate, banks may either:

  • Increase EMI amount, or
  • Increase loan tenure

Most banks prefer extending tenure rather than increasing EMI.

Which is Better in India (2026)?

In India, most home loan borrowers prefer floating rates because RBI policy changes frequently. However, if you want financial peace of mind, fixed rate can be safer.
