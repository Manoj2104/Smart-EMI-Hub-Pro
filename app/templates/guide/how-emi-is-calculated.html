What is EMI? Complete 2026 Guide to Equated Monthly Installment in India

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is the fixed monthly payment a borrower makes to repay a loan over a specified tenure. It includes both principal repayment and interest charged by the lender. Understanding EMI is essential before taking any loan such as home loan, personal loan, car loan or business loan.

1. EMI Meaning Explained in Depth

When you borrow money from a bank, you agree to repay it over time instead of in one lump sum. The bank calculates a fixed monthly payment amount based on loan amount, interest rate and tenure. This monthly payment is EMI.

4. Factors That Affect EMI

  • Loan Amount
  • Interest Rate
  • Loan Tenure
  • Credit Score
  • Type of Interest (Fixed/Floating)

Higher loan amount or interest leads to higher EMI.

5. How Banks Calculate EMI in India

Banks use automated systems based on reducing balance formula. They also consider borrower income, CIBIL score, employment type and debt ratio.

EMI approval also depends on Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio. Ideally EMI should not exceed 40% of monthly income.

6. How to Reduce EMI Legally

  • Increase loan tenure
  • Make partial prepayment
  • Negotiate lower interest
  • Transfer balance to lower rate bank
